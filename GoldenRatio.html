The Golden Ratio

The Golden Ratio, also known as Phi (Î¦), is a unique number that has fascinated mathematicians, artists, and architects for centuries. It appears in various natural phenomena, art, architecture, and even in the design of certain financial models. The ratio is approximately 1.618 and can be observed in the arrangement of leaves on a stem, the spiral of seashells, and the layout of famous buildings.

In this section, we will explore the mathematical properties of the Golden Ratio, its historical significance, and how it can be applied to modern design.
